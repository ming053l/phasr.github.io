PhaSR: Generalized Image Shadow Removal with Physically Aligned Priors

Chia-Ming Lee1,2, Yu-Fan Lin2, Yu-Jou Hsiao1, Jing-Hui Jung2, Yu-Lun Liu1, Chih-Chung Hsu1,2
1National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University, 2National Cheng Kung University
Paper arXiv Code Data

PhaSR combines parameter-free Retinex normalization with geometric-semantic cross-modal attention for state-of-the-art shadow removal and ambient lighting normalization with highest efficiency.

Abstract

Shadow removal under diverse lighting conditions requires disentangling illumination from intrinsic reflectance—a challenge compounded when physical priors are not properly aligned.

We propose PhaSR (Physically Aligned Shadow Removal), addressing this through dual-level prior alignment to enable robust performance from single-light shadows to multi-source ambient lighting. First, Physically Aligned Normalization (PAN) performs closed-form illumination correction via Gray-world normalization, log-domain Retinex decomposition, and dynamic range recombination, suppressing chromatic bias. Second, Geometric-Semantic Rectification Attention (GSRA) extends differential attention to cross-modal alignment, harmonizing depth-derived geometry with DINO-v2 semantic embeddings to resolve modal conflicts under varying illumination.

Experiments show competitive performance in shadow removal with lower complexity and generalization to ambient lighting where traditional methods fail under multi-source illumination.

Overview Pipeline

Our proposed network. PhaSR achieves physical alignment through two stages: (1) PAN performs model-free illumination normalization via Gray-world correction and log-domain Retinex decomposition, and (2) a multi-scale Transformer encoder-decoder integrates DINO-v2 semantic embeddings and DepthAnything-v2 geometric priors through GSRA's cross-modal differential attention (A_rect = A_sem - λ·A_geo), enabling robust reflectance recovery from single-light shadows to multi-source ambient lighting.
